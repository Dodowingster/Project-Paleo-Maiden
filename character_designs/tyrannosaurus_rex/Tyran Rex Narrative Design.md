> ### 📜 The Montana Sting & The Name of Tyrant
> Before enrolling at the Stratum Institute, Tyran was a kidnapped child forced to fight as a novelty attraction for a criminal syndicate in Montana. Possessing boundless stamina and a massive pool of Paleo-Magic, she found a feral joy in the arena and simply could not lose. Her tyrannical, merciless fighting style earned her the nickname "Tyran". Everything changed when she was soundly defeated by a mysterious robed opponent wielding a P-Magic greatsword. That opponent was Coelurosa Rex—the former World Cup Champion working undercover alongside high-ranking officer Barnum Bones to raid the Syndicate. After the ring was dismantled, Coelurosa recommended the raw rookie to Headmaster Buckland. To honor the woman who saved her and gave her a second chance, Tyran adopted the surname **Rex**.

## 2. Campaign Prologue: The Sovereign Proving Ground

### The Last Resort Option

The campaign begins with a heated faculty board meeting at the Stratum Institute. The elite, veteran lecturers have completely run out of options for Tyran Rex. Her staggering, record-breaking capabilities, bottomless stamina, and raw brute force entirely outscale standard academic training methods, leaving the senior staff lacking the skill or knowledge to teach her. Out of desperation, the board suggests assigning the **Lecturer (the player)**—a newcomer fresh to the field—reasoning that an unconventional mind might yield a different result. However, Headmaster Buckland is firmly against it, refusing to risk a newcomer's career on the academy's most chaotic asset.

### The Unapproachable King

Following the meeting, the unassigned Lecturer takes a stroll around the massive mobile campus. They happen upon an outdoor training field where Tyran Rex is standing completely alone, actively pacing back and forth over a stack of messy paperwork and a discarded map. A wide, empty perimeter surrounds her; ordinary students and senior staff members are actively avoiding the field, entirely intimidated by her terrifying reputation and rumors of her brutal underworld history. Nobody dares to go near her.

### The Audacity to Approach

Breaking the unwritten rule of the campus, the Lecturer walks straight across the empty perimeter with zero hesitation and approaches the 193 cm powerhouse directly. Tyran snaps out of her thoughts, her eyes lighting up. Completely unaware of the crushing, intimidating aura she is radiating, she greets the newcomer with a booming, overly enthusiastic laugh and a wide grin, firmly believing she is being incredibly charming and welcoming. Instead of flinching or keeping a fearful distance like the other faculty members, the Lecturer stands perfectly composed and asks her a simple, direct question: *"What's the issue you're having, President?"*

### The Overlord's Dilemma

Tyran sighs heavily, letting her loud bravado drop into a pout as she explains her dilemma: she just had a massive disagreement with her Vice President, Horri Tricera. Tyran wanted to implement an intense, high-stakes training initiative to "motivate" the junior students, but Horri completely shut it down, calling it reckless and refusing to sign off on the paperwork. Tyran genuinely wants to inspire the next generation to be great fighters, but she is frustrated that her unyielding approach keeps clashing with Horri's immovable, structured methods.

### The Oath of a Mentor

The Lecturer looks over the notes and offers a piece of genuine, calm advice. They explain that a true Apex leader doesn't just drive others forward by force; they understand that an unstoppable force needs an immovable object to keep the foundation from collapsing. Horri isn't trying to stifle Tyran's inspiration; she is trying to build a safe, structured environment so the students don't break under the weight of Tyran's massive standards. To lead them effectively, Tyran needs to channel her bottomless stamina into collaboration, rather than trying to crush the disagreement.

Tyran processes the advice, the brilliant tactical and diplomatic value of the words clicking in her mind. She looks at the Lecturer with a mixture of awe and deep curiosity.

> *"You're a strange one, Professor. Everyone else usually gives me a wide berth or agrees with whatever I say just to get out of the room. How come you didn't walk away like the others?"*

The Lecturer looks her dead in the eye, perfectly steady, and replies:

> *"As long as you bear the symbol of this institute, you are my student. I will do my best to aid you in achieving your noble goal. That is my oath towards the institute."*

Tyran stares at the Lecturer for a quiet second, a massive, genuine, and proud smile breaking across her face.

> *"Thank you, Professor! You really are cut from different cloth. I learned something today!"*

### The Sovereign's Decree

Later, the board is gathered in the meeting room again. Headmaster Buckland is about to reluctantly appoint yet another senior, veteran lecturer to handle Tyran Rex when the doors suddenly fly open. Tyran bolts directly into the room, catching the entire board by surprise. Completely bypassing the traditional bureaucratic process, she loudly and confidently proposes the player as her designated lecturer for the upcoming Great Expedition. She declares to the stunned room that this new Lecturer has exactly what it takes to guide her, and she is eager to learn from them. Buckland, recognizing the absolute, unyielding determination in his Student Council President, finally yields to her choice.

## 4. MAX Affinity Camp Event: "The Cost of Solitude"

**Prerequisite:** Max Affinity reached with the Lecturer prior to the Cretaceous Grand Finals.

* **The Atmosphere**: Late at night in the central courtyard of the training camp. Tyran sits cross-legged on her own bulky tail using it as a makeshift cushion, meticulously polishing the edge of her P-Magic greatsword.
* **The Dialogue**: She stops her rhythmic polishing, looking up at the stars with an uncharacteristic, quiet pensiveness before addressing the Lecturer:
> *"Hey, Lecturer... do you remember the first day we met on the training field? When you actually walked right up to me?"*

She admits that despite her loud bravado and her firm belief that she is an inspiring, charming leader, she isn't entirely blind. She notices the look in the eyes of the ordinary students she tries to mentor—the pale, frozen fear rather than the fiery inspiration she intends to spark. For the first time, she opens up about her childhood in the Montana underground rings, explaining that when you grow up in a slave cage where loud commands and absolute brute force are the only things keeping you alive, you forget how to speak any other language.

Dropping her shield entirely, she looks at her greatsword with quiet vulnerability:
> "Everyone keeps their distance because they think I'm too much to handle. The students look at me like a monster, and the teachers treat me like a problem without a solution. For the longest time, I thought... maybe this is just the tax you pay. Maybe absolute power automatically sentences you to a lifetime of total solitude. But you... you didn't look at the warning signs. You didn't run away. You just looked me in the eye and saw a student who wanted to learn."

* **The Temporal Anomaly**: As she stands up and lifts her greatsword toward the night sky, her massive P-Magic pool naturally flares in absolute, peak-affinity synchronization with the Lecturer's presence. The golden-red aura radiating from her body doesn't just illuminate the courtyard; it exerts a heavy, localized gravitational pull that causes the ambient pebbles to levitate. For a split second, a massive, phantasmal crown of pure prehistoric energy manifests above her head, symbolizing the undisputed apex authority of the Late Cretaceous era. Turning back to the Lecturer with her signature, confident grin, the anomaly vanishes as she says:
> *"You didn't look at me like an unruly underworld beast that needed a leash. You looked at me like a King who just needed a partner. Let's go break some records tomorrow, Professor."*

## 5. The Paradox Closed: True Endgame Continuity

In the **Gauntlet Mode True Ending**, upon learning the devastating truth that the Lecturer's mind is violently wiped of all memories at the conclusion of every single time loop, Tyran's motivation evolves from a quest for personal glory into an act of supreme, chosen sovereignty.

Refusing to allow her mentor to be continuously hollowed out by the timeline's resets, Tyran issues her **Sovereign Continuity Decree**. She intercepts the chronological feedback loop and binds its repeating nature directly to her own eternal reign, intentionally trapping herself within the recurrence space so that the Lecturer and the rest of the student body can finally cross the threshold into a continuous, linear future.

By transforming the cyclical return of the unresolvable anomaly into her own permanent, personal battlefield, she willingly surrenders the chance at a normal, peaceful retirement. She becomes the absolute, standing vanguard against the cosmic reset. For eternity, whenever the temporal alarm bells toll across the boundaries of reality, Tyran Rex simply smiles, lifts her greatsword against the resetting sky, and gladly welcomes the endless challenge—forever embodying the true weight, duty, and sacrifice of the Tyrant King.
