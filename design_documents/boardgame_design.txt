================================================================================
PALEO-MAIDEN CHAMPIONSHIP: BOARD GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT
================================================================================
Last Updated: May 5, 2026

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME IDENTITY PILLARS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All design decisions should be evaluated against these three pillars:

  1. CHARACTERIZATION THROUGH HISTORICAL ACCURACY
     - Characters are grounded in real paleontological data (species traits,
       biome habitats, physical attributes)
     - Mechanics should reflect the real-world nature of each species

  2. EMPHASIS ON MAKING CHARACTERS LOOK COOL
     - Every meaningful event should produce a narrative/visual moment, not
       just a stat change
     - Players should feel like they are watching their Maiden grow and evolve

  3. FIGHTING GAME INFLUENCE
     - Combat, technique inheritance, and build decisions should feel like
       a fighting game player developing and locking in their main's gameplan
     - Rivalry, rematches, and technique stealing are core to the identity

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CORE LOOP OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - 3-year run, 24 turns per year
  - Board game world map with dice-driven non-linear movement
  - Combat resolved by the auto-battler (Godot prototype)
  - Failure to qualify in a year's final tournament ends the run permanently
  - At the end of the run, the player's Final Build is locked permanently
    and added to their Maiden Build Gallery (collection across all runs)

NAVIGATION SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Navigation is driven by expedition intelligence, not abstract luck.
  Before moving, the player rolls a SCOUT ROLL to represent the quality of
  field intel gathered — local rumors, terrain reports, fossil indicators,
  and sightings from other expeditions.

  SCOUT ROLL:
  The result determines how many spaces ahead the expedition can read.
  The player inspects all spaces within that range, then chooses ONE to
  travel to as their destination.
  - High roll = strong lead, wide range of options visible and reachable.
  - Low roll  = vague intel, fewer options available this turn.
  The player is not forced to travel the full rolled distance. Choosing a
  closer destination reduces the ration cost.

  COST: Travel consumes INGREDIENTS as rations, not P-Magic. Cost is based
  on the distance to the chosen destination:
   - 1-2 spaces = spend 1 ingredient
   - 3-4 spaces = spend 2 ingredients
   - 5-6 spaces = spend 3 ingredients

  RATION CHOICE AND AFFINITY:
  The ingredient(s) spent are the expedition's travel rations for the turn.
  Each Maiden has food preferences, so the ration type can slightly affect
  Affinity:
   - Preferred ration → small Affinity gain
   - Neutral ration   → no Affinity change
   - Disliked ration  → small Affinity loss
  This is intentionally a small effect. Cooking remains the primary way to
  build Affinity through food.

  AUTOMATIC YIELD DURING MOVEMENT:
  Ingredients and Excavation Finds are gathered automatically from spaces
  passed through en route to the chosen destination. Yield scales with
  distance traveled — longer journeys produce more resources.

  INGREDIENTS (personal survival economy):
   - Lush biomes (Forest, Coastal, Plains): high yield
   - Harsh biomes (Desert, Volcanic, Mountain, Tundra): low yield

  EXCAVATION FINDS (academic/Institute economy):
   - Harsh biomes (Desert, Volcanic, Mountain, Tundra): high yield —
     rich fossil deposits, geological sites
   - Lush biomes: low yield — poor fossil record

  Biome yield ratios:
   Forest / Coastal  → Ingredients ↑↑  Finds ↓
   Plains            → Ingredients ↑   Finds ↑
   Desert / Volcanic → Ingredients ↓   Finds ↑↑
   Mountain / Tundra → Ingredients ↓   Finds ↑↑

  NOTE: Harsh biomes are historically accurate — real-world fossil deposits
  are concentrated in arid environments (Patagonia, Gobi Desert, etc.).

COOK SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  INGREDIENT SUBTYPES:
  Navigation yields typed ingredients, not a generic count. Subtype is
  determined by the biome traveled through:
  ┌────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Ingredient     │ Primary Biomes                                        │
  ├────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Meat           │ Plains, Volcanic, Mountain (megafauna, prey animals)  │
  ├────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Plant          │ Forest, Plains (herbivore staples, roots, leaves)     │
  ├────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Seafood        │ Coastal (fish, crustaceans, aquatic fauna)            │
  ├────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Fungi/Forage   │ Forest, Tundra (mushrooms, gathered roots, lichens)   │
  └────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
  NOTE: A biome can yield multiple subtypes at lower rates. The table above
  represents primary yields only.

  NAMED DISH SYSTEM:
  Specific ingredient combinations unlock named dishes. Each Maiden has a
  personal preference table defining how she reacts to each dish:
  - Loved    → P-Magic ↑↑ + Affinity ↑↑↑ + one technique from her own pool
  - Liked    → P-Magic ↑↑ + Affinity ↑↑
  - Neutral  → base Cook reward (P-Magic ↑ + Affinity ↑)
  - Disliked → P-Magic ↑ only, no Affinity gain

  Cook is intentionally a larger relationship boost than travel rationing.
  Navigation rations can nudge Affinity slightly, but Cook is the deliberate
  bonding action that produces meaningful Affinity growth.

  The technique reward on a Loved dish is drawn from the Maiden's OWN
  discipline pool — it reflects her being genuinely restored and focused,
  not learning from an opponent.

  AUTHORING REQUIREMENT (per Maiden):
  - 1-2 Loved dishes (specific combinations + named dish title)
  - 1-2 Liked dishes
  - Disliked category (ingredient type or combination she dislikes)
  - 1 reaction line per tier (Loved / Liked / Disliked)
  Paleontological accuracy applies: herbivore Maidens should dislike
  meat-heavy dishes; carnivore Maidens should dislike pure-plant dishes.

CURRENCY SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Two separate resource currencies exist, representing different aspects of
  the Great Expedition:

  INGREDIENTS
  - Gathered automatically via Navigate (surface-level exploration)
  - Personal survival economy
  - Spent on: Navigation rations, Cook, Tier 1-2 Barter items, Arena Shop
    (Tier 1-2)

  EXCAVATION FINDS
  - Gathered automatically via Navigate (subsurface geological/fossil sites)
  - Academic/Institute economy
  - Spent on: Tier 3 Barter items, Arena Shop (Tier 3), Institute
    contributions (KIV)
  - Harsh biomes yield the most — incentivizes risky routing

  KIV: Submitting Excavation Finds to the Stratum Institute may provide
  a separate benefit (reputation meter, research upgrades, Title Match
  entry fees). Not yet designed.

ARENA SHOP (Fixed Shops at Title Match Spaces)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Title Match spaces (Arena spaces) double as FIXED SHOPS with guaranteed,
  predetermined stock — unlike the rotating randomness of the Field Shop.

  KEY DIFFERENCES FROM FIELD SHOP:
  ┌─────────────────────┬──────────────────────┬──────────────────────────┐
  │                     │ Field Shop (Barter)  │ Arena Shop               │
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Stock               │ Random (2-3 items)   │ Fixed, guaranteed        │
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Refresh             │ Every 2 turns        │ Static per location      │
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Availability        │ Any camp (Wilderness)│ Must land on Arena space │
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Currency            │ Ingredients / Finds  │ Ingredients / Finds      │
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Delivery            │ Delayed (next turn)  │ Immediate (on-site)      │
  └─────────────────────┴──────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┘

  OUT OF STOCK SYSTEM:
  - If a player arrives at an Arena Shop and a Tier 3 item is out of stock,
    a specific named Paleo Maiden is responsible — she got there first and
    bought it out.
  - The culprit Maiden is displayed to the player with a short line of
    dialogue (e.g. Nevesi smugly holding your Local Map).
  - This grounds item scarcity in characterization rather than anonymous RNG
    and creates memorable "she STOLE my item" moments.
  - Challenge them to a fight for the item.

  NOTE: The harder-to-reach nature of Arena spaces (predetermined fixed
  locations on the map) makes guaranteed stock a meaningful reward for
  deliberate routing.

ENCOUNTER & INTERACTION SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  STEP 1 — SPAWN
  When landing on a Wilderness space, there is a chance a named Paleo Maiden
  from the roster spawns in camp. The spawning Maiden is drawn from the pool
  of maidens whose home biome matches the current space's biome.

  TENSION WEIGHTING ON APPEARANCE:
  - Higher Tension with a specific Maiden increases her appearance weight.
  - Low Tension = baseline spawn chance.
  - Mid/High Tension = progressively higher chance to appear when biome
    eligibility is met.
  - Design goal: rivalry momentum should feel alive during a run.

  DESIGNATED RIVAL EXCEPTION:
  - All Player Maidens have a fixed designated rival in their career run.
  - Example: Tyran Rex's designated rival is Horri Tricera.
  - Designated rivals are NOT treated as normal random camp spawns.
  - They are scheduled through Rival Interaction beats (milestones) and
    may also be used as Crossroads anchors.
  - This keeps their relationship consistently present across the run,
    instead of depending on RNG camp encounter luck.
  - Fixed designated rivals coexist with player-selected extra rivals.
    The fixed rival is guaranteed by default; extra rivals can be added
    during the run to shape finals matchups and inheritance targets.

  VOCATION SHIFT (Year 3 prevalence):
  - In Years 1 and 2, encountered maidens appear in their base vocation.
  - In Year 3, maidens may appear in a biome-shifted vocation more commonly.
    Example: Aegypti Spino normally = Focus/Semi-Aquatic.
             Aegypti Spino in Volcanic biome = Brute/Focus shifted vocation.
  - This reflects the lore concept of evolution and mastery — the maiden has
    been adapting to the environment, creating a new hybrid discipline form.
  - The shifted vocation is NOT revealed to the player before engaging.
    The player sees only that a Maiden has appeared in camp — the vocation
    shift is discovered mid-sparring, preserving the surprise and making
    the encounter feel like a genuine fighting game "who is this version"
    moment.

  VOCATION SHIFT EFFECT ON PLAYER'S MAIDEN:
  - When the PLAYER'S Maiden unlocks a hybrid vocation, her biome
    affinities expand to include the biomes associated with the new
    discipline.
  - Example: Sastrei Taurus (base = Flow/Plains) unlocks Boxing Magus
    (Flow + Focus). Focus is associated with Coastal biomes. She now
    has home-ground advantage in BOTH Plains and Coastal.
  - This affects: Train costs, Navigate yields, and passive dialogue lines.
    Her dialogue should reflect the adaptation — she no longer complains
    about previously uncomfortable biomes once her vocation covers them.
  - Biome affinity per discipline (reference):
    Brute   → Volcanic, Mountain
    Focus   → Coastal, Tundra
    Flow    → Plains, Forest
    Control → Desert

  STEP 2 — INTERACT
  The player chooses to Interact with the present Maiden.
  An interaction scene is drawn randomly from that Maiden's interaction pool.

  INTERACTION TRACKS:
  - CAMP INTERACTIONS (random):
    Uses the normal encounter flow above. Triggered by random camp spawns.
    Intended for broad roster variety, casual social texture, and emergent
    pairings.
  - RIVAL INTERACTIONS (fixed):
    Used for designated rival relationships. Triggered at planned points
    in the run (milestones/Crossroads/scheduled beats), not pure camp RNG.
    Intended for high-importance relationship progression and rematch setup.

  INTERACTION POOL RULES:
  - Each named Maiden has a minimum of 3 unique interaction scenes.
  - Each scene is drawn without replacement — once an interaction plays,
    it is removed from the pool for the remainder of that run.
  - If the pool is exhausted, repeat encounters with that Maiden produce
    a neutral "passing encounter" with no scene.

  INTERACTION DESIGN PHILOSOPHY:
  There are NO generic interaction types. Every interaction is a uniquely
  authored scene specific to the relationship between your Maiden and the
  Maiden encountered. Two Maidens who have a rivalry produce entirely
  different scenes than two Maidens who are strangers or old friends.

  SCENE KEYING RULE:
  Interaction content is keyed by the PAIR, not only by the encountered
  Maiden. In practice, this means:
  - Tyran Rex (player) + Horri Tricera (encountered rival) uses one scene set
  - Sastrei Taurus (player) + Horri Tricera (encountered maiden) uses a
    different scene set
  The encountered Maiden may be the same, but the relationship context is
  different, so scenes and outcomes must be different.

  Each scene is written from scratch — the sparring outcome (present or
  absent, and its branching results) is determined by the scene itself,
  not by a category label. A given scene either has sparring built into
  it or it does not. A given scene's win/loss outcomes are specific to
  the emotional stakes of that scene, not a shared mechanical template.

  AUTHORING REQUIREMENTS (per interaction scene):
  - Named relationship or scenario (serves as internal design shorthand)
  - Clear emotional premise: what does this scene reveal about these two?
  - Sparring: present or absent (determined by scene logic)
  - If sparring present: separate WIN branch and LOSS branch, each with
    distinct dialogue and mechanical outcomes
  - If no sparring: the scene presents a PLAYER CHOICE. The choice is
    framed by the scene's emotional context (not a generic menu).
    Each option leads to a different resolution and a different reward:
     • One option typically yields a technique from the encountered
       Maiden's pool (the player engages through the relationship
       between the two Maidens — observing, learning)
     • One option typically yields a P-Magic restore (the player steps
       in directly, usually in a quieter, more personal context)
    Both options should feel like genuine expressions of the scene's
    premise — neither is a "correct" answer. The scene's outcome
    (what actually happens in the world) may be identical regardless
    of choice; the reward reflects HOW the player engaged, not WHETHER
    they succeeded.

  RIVAL TRACK EXAMPLE — Player Maiden = Tyran Rex,
  Designated Rival = Horri Tricera:

  Perspective note:
  - In this sample, you are playing a run with Tyran Rex as your chosen
    Maiden.
  - Horri Tricera is Tyran Rex's fixed designated rival for this run.
  - These scenes are RIVAL INTERACTIONS (scheduled beats), not random
    CAMP INTERACTIONS.

  Scene 1 — "My Most Respected Rival"
    Premise: Tyran Rex and Horri Tricera have a mutual, deeply-felt respect.
    Both recognize something in the other that they themselves lack.
    The gap is felt but neither can articulate it cleanly — a sparring
    session is the only honest language they share.
    Sparring: Guaranteed.
    WIN branch (Tyran Rex wins): Tyran Rex finally understands what she
      has been missing. A moment of genuine clarity. Horri Tricera
      acknowledges the loss but leaves still searching — her question
      is not yet answered.
    LOSS branch (Tyran Rex loses): Horri Tricera finally sees what she
      lacked. Tyran Rex loses but does not diminish — she watches
      Horri Tricera walk away and keeps searching too.

  Scene 2 — "The Tyrant King"
    Premise: Tyran Rex is Student Council President and believes she is
    deeply respected and valued by the student body. She is entirely
    genuine about this — she shares this pride openly with the player.
    In truth, the students obey out of fear, not admiration. Horri
    Tricera knows this and tries to gently tell her. Tyran Rex does not
    get the hint. The scene ends with her still completely oblivious.
    Sparring: None.
    Tonal register: Not mockery of Tyran Rex — something more bittersweet.
    A person whose strength has blinded her to how she is truly seen.
    Player Choice:
     Option A — Encourage Horri Tricera to tell her directly:
       Horri Tricera tries again, more plainly this time. Tyran Rex
       receives it warmly, misses the point entirely, and thanks Horri
       Tricera for the kind words. Horri Tricera gives up.
       Reward: One random technique from Horri Tricera's Control pool
       added to your library. (The interaction played out through the
       relationship between these two — you observed and learned.)
     Option B — Tell Tyran Rex yourself, over dinner:
       A quieter moment. You try during a meal together. She listens,
       nods earnestly, and interprets everything you said as proof of
       how much her students care. She leaves in high spirits.
       Reward: P-Magic restored. (A meal, a conversation — she didn't
       hear you, but the effort cost you something and you recover.)

  STEP 3 — SCENE-DRIVEN OUTCOMES (NO GLOBAL FLOW TEMPLATE)

  There is no universal post-sparring flow chart.
  Outcome logic is authored per interaction scene and per Maiden pair.

  AUTHORING RULES FOR OUTCOMES:
  - If a scene includes sparring, write win/loss outcomes specifically for
    that scene's emotional context.
  - If a scene includes non-sparring choices, write option outcomes
    specifically for that scene's context.
  - Presence/absence of the encountered Maiden after the scene is not fixed
    globally; it is scene-defined.
  - Reward logic is scene-defined, but must follow these guardrails:
    * Sparring wins are the primary source of technique inheritance.
    * Non-sparring options may grant technique or P-Magic when justified by
      the scene premise (for example: inspiration from observing another
      Maiden's style, or recovery from personal conversation).
    * Loss outcomes should still provide meaningful character progression,
      even when rewards are lower than a win.

  BALANCE GUARDRAIL:
  - Across a full run, sparring should remain the dominant and most reliable
    way to expand the player's technique library.

  DIALOGUE REQUIREMENTS PER MAIDEN:
  - Dialogue content is authored per Maiden-pair scene, not from a
    universal post-fight template.
  - If a scene contains sparring, write reaction lines for both outcomes
    in each character's voice.
  - If a scene contains choices, write both option branches in voice and
    make the emotional difference legible.
  - Lecturer response lines should reflect the pair dynamic and the
    current run state.

  SIGNATURE SKILL CHANCE ON WIN:
  - Winning a sparring session has a chance to add the encountered Maiden's
    Signature Skill to the player's library instead of a generic technique.
  - The probability of the Signature Skill appearing scales with the
    encountered Maiden's Tension level (see KIV: Tension System below).
  - EXCEPTION: Winning a match by intentionally landing on a Tournament
    Space GUARANTEES the Signature Skill is learned. No RNG.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PASSIVE DIALOGUE SYSTEM (Ambient Character Voice)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspired by FF8's floating NPC dialogue, the player's Maiden delivers
  short contextual lines during non-encounter turns. Lines are NOT
  interactive — they appear as floating text/speech bubbles and require
  no player input. They exist purely as texture to make every run feel
  distinct and to communicate characterization passively.

  THREE TRIGGER CATEGORIES:

  1. BIOME TRAVEL LINES
     Triggered when moving through or landing on a biome space.
     Familiar biomes → confident, comfortable.
     Unfamiliar/harsh biomes → irritated, challenged.
     These also function as a soft tutorial — players learn a Maiden's
     biome affinities through personality before reading any mechanic.

  2. NAVIGATION RESULT LINES
      Triggered when Navigate resolves at the end of movement.
      Covers ration reactions, yield quality, and whether a technique was
      found.

  3. SHOP / BARTER LINES
     Triggered when the Barter menu is opened or when browsing the
     Arena Shop. Includes item-specific hint lines and out-of-stock
     culprit reactions (per named Maiden responsible).

  ── WRITING REQUIREMENTS PER MAIDEN ────────────────────────────────────
  Each Maiden requires:
  - 2-3 lines per biome (familiar / neutral / harsh)
  - 2-3 navigation result lines (ration reaction / good yield / bad yield /
    technique found)
  - 3-4 barter/shop lines (general browse, item-specific, impatient)
  - 1 out-of-stock reaction line per named culprit Maiden
  Lines should be one sentence maximum. Voice must be consistent with
  the Maiden's established personality.

  AFFINITY INFLUENCE ON DIALOGUE:
  A Maiden's passive dialogue lines shift in tone depending on the current
  Affinity level. The same trigger may produce a different line at Low,
  Mid, and High Affinity:
  - Low Affinity  → distant, guarded, minimal. She doesn't volunteer much.
  - Mid Affinity  → her natural voice. The baseline lines.
  - High Affinity → warmer, lets her guard down. Things she'd normally
                    cut off, she finishes. Personal details slip through.
  Each line set should be written with all three tiers in mind.

  Personality: Terse, clipped, practically minded. Talks like she's
  always mid-focus and the conversation is mildly interrupting her.
  Warm undertones she doesn't advertise.

CROSSROADS SPACE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Status: DECIDED.

  Crossroads spaces are rare world events encountered while traveling
  (during Navigation or en route to a destination). They surface Rival Interaction
  scenes from NON-PLAYER Maiden pairs — scenes the player's Maiden was never
  part of, witnessed from the outside.

  CORE CONCEPT:
  - Crossroads events draw directly from the existing Rival Interaction
    scene pool of other Maiden pairs. No separate scene authoring required.
  - Example: Player is running Sastrei Taurus. A Crossroads event triggers
    "My Most Respected Rival" — the Tyran Rex × Horri Tricera scene —
    playing out in front of her.
  - The player chooses whether to watch (and stay for the sparring, if one
    occurs) or move on.
  - Watching can inspire Sastrei and contribute a technique to her pool
    (scene-defined reward, drawn from the observed Maiden's discipline).

  KEY DESIGN IMPLICATION:
  - Every Rival Interaction scene is authored once per Maiden pair.
  - That same scene can be experienced directly (when running that Maiden)
    OR observed as a Crossroads event (when running a different Maiden).
  - This means zero additional authoring cost for Crossroads specifically.

  ROLE VS CAMP ENCOUNTERS:
  - Camp encounters = direct interaction between player's Maiden and an
    encountered Maiden.
  - Crossroads = observational interactions between non-player Maiden pairs
    that still influence the player's run through inspiration and world context.

  DESIGN GOAL:
  Crossroads makes the world feel alive independent of the player while still
  feeding build progression and characterization. Scenes that Sastrei would
  never be part of still matter to her run.

  KIV: Authoring scope for all Maiden pair scene sets is tracked separately.

KIV: TENSION SYSTEM (Keep In View — Not Yet Designed)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  CONCEPT:
  - A separate hidden/visible Tension tracker per encountered named Maiden
    (distinct from Affinity, which tracks Lecturer ↔ Your Maiden relationship)
  - Tension covers both rivalry and admiration — it is the weight of history
    between your Maiden and another, regardless of whether it is warm or hostile
  - High Tension increases that Maiden's appearance frequency during the run
    (weighted spawn priority and stronger presence in encounter pacing)
  - Built through: repeat camp encounters, sparring wins/losses,
    Crossroads scenes
  - At thresholds, the player may nominate multiple selected rivals for the
    current run.

  RIVAL SELECTION (MULTI-RIVAL):
  - Each player Maiden has one fixed designated rival from lore.
    Example: Sastrei Taurus ↔ Nevesi Pycnonemo.
  - During a run, the player may add extra selected rivals (based on Tension)
    to target specific inheritance paths and finals matchups.
  - Selected rivals are guaranteed to appear in the final league/tournament
    bracket for that run.
  - This guarantees access to those rivals' unique inheritance opportunities
    if the player reaches and wins those key matches.
  - Example build routing: If building Boxing Magus Sastrei Taurus, the
    player may select Aegypti Spino or Kitadani Fukuira as additional
    rivals so they are guaranteed finals opponents for desired uniques.

  WHY THIS IS IMPORTANT:
  - Serves the characterization pillar: rivalry emerges from play, not
    cutscenes
  - Gives replayability: different runs with the same Maiden can produce
    different rival sets and inheritance routes
  - Gives players a personal stake in the tournament ("I want the rematch
    with Nevesi because WE have a history")

AFFINITY (CLARIFICATION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Affinity = the bond between the PLAYER (Lecturer) and THEIR CHOSEN MAIDEN.
  It is NOT a relationship meter with encountered rival maidens.

  Built through:
  - Cooking meals in camp (major gains)
  - Preferred travel rations during Navigation (minor gains)
  - Successful camp actions
  - Positive interaction outcomes

  Effects:
  - Higher Affinity = better combat performance / morale for your Maiden
  - May affect unlock conditions for certain camp actions or scenes

  The Tension system (KIV above) is a SEPARATE tracker and should not
  be conflated with Affinity.
